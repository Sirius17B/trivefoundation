Privacy

Privacy Policy

How TriveFoundation protects enquiries, donor details, participant data, media, and admin-managed content.

Last updated: 14 June 2026. This policy should be reviewed by counsel before production launch.

What we collect

We only request information needed to respond to enquiries, coordinate volunteers or sponsors, acknowledge donations, run programmes, and protect participants. This may include name, email, interest type, message content, donation reference details that a donor chooses to share, quiz/leaderboard display names, and media consent records.

Donor privacy

Donation records must never be published with full bank references, phone numbers, email addresses, or private notes. Public acknowledgements should use an opt-in display name such as “Anonymous”, first name only, or an organisation name approved by the donor.

How donor identity should be protected

  • Collect donor confirmations through a secure backend form, not public email threads or browser-only storage.
  • Store donor identity separately from public impact totals and use an internal donor ID for reconciliation.
  • Encrypt donor records at rest, restrict access by role, and keep an audit log of every view or export.
  • Allow anonymous giving and require explicit consent before publishing any donor name.
  • Delete or anonymise donor records when they are no longer needed for acknowledgement, accounting, or legal retention.

Local storage and admin tools

The current static website can store some admin edits or quiz results in the browser. That storage is not a secure database and should not hold private donor identities, participant personal data, or confidential admin credentials.

Analytics and cookies

The site should avoid non-essential tracking by default. If analytics or advertising pixels are added later, TriveFoundation should publish the provider names, purpose, retention period, and any consent controls required by applicable law.
